CURATOR'S NOTES

Patterns Across the Halls

What the individual failures add up to — the cross-cutting lessons a single exhibit can't show.

  1. The model believes whatever the harness feeds it.

    Silent garbage quotes, misclassified errors, sections that overflow their boundary — on the surface they read as "hallucination", but the root cause lives in the tool layer. Verify tool output before blaming model behavior.

    HE-03 · HE-04 · CE-03
  2. Prompt-harden, then pin with an eval.

    PE-01 gave birth to the entire eval framework. Every failure fixed since leaves behind a scorer, a regression assertion, or a contract test. A fix without an eval isn't finished.

    PE-01 · PE-04
  3. Format control needs a two-sided pincer.

    Tighten the prompt and widen the parser. Locking output format by prompt alone always drifts under real traffic — downstream must tolerate what the model actually emits.

    PE-02 · PE-05
