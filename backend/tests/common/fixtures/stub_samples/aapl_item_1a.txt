Item 1A. Risk Factors

The Company's business, financial condition, and results of operations are subject to a number of risks and uncertainties, many of which are outside of the Company's control. The following risk factors, among others, could cause actual results to differ materially from those projected in any forward-looking statement.

Global and regional economic conditions could materially adversely affect the Company. The Company's operations and performance depend significantly on global and regional economic conditions. Adverse macroeconomic conditions, including inflation, slower growth, recession, changes in interest rates, tighter monetary policy, and geopolitical instability, can negatively affect consumer confidence and discretionary spending, reducing demand for the Company's products and services.

The markets for the Company's products and services are highly competitive, and the Company faces substantial competition in all areas of its business. Competitors vary in size and in the breadth of products and services they offer, and many pursue aggressive pricing strategies and cost reductions. The Company's ability to compete successfully depends heavily on its ability to ensure a continuing and timely introduction of innovative new products, services, and technologies to the marketplace.

The Company relies on a complex global supply chain to manufacture and deliver its products. Disruptions in the supply chain, whether caused by natural disasters, public health emergencies, labor disputes, political unrest, trade restrictions, or the failure of key suppliers, could impair the Company's ability to meet customer demand and could increase manufacturing costs.

The Company is subject to complex and changing laws and regulations worldwide, including those related to antitrust, privacy, data protection, consumer protection, product safety, environmental protection, labor, trade, tax, and anti-corruption. New or changing regulatory requirements, or enforcement actions, could result in significant fines, operating restrictions, or reputational harm that materially and adversely affect the Company's business.

Cybersecurity threats and information security incidents could compromise the Company's systems, disrupt operations, and damage customer relationships. The Company's products, services, and internal systems face continuous attempts at intrusion, and a successful attack could lead to loss of confidential information, unauthorized access to customer data, extended service outages, and significant remediation costs.
